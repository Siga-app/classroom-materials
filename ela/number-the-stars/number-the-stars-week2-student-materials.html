Poetry Rubric
Criteria 1, Beginning 2, Approaching 3, Meeting 4, Exceeding
Speaker and Perspective The speaker is unclear or missing. The speaker is somewhat clear but needs more development. The speaker is clear and connected to the novel or unit. The speaker is clear, purposeful, and emotionally convincing.
Poetic Craft The poem reads mostly like a paragraph broken into lines. A poetic device is attempted but the effect is limited. At least one poetic device is used intentionally. Repetition, line breaks, and word choice create a strong effect.
Spelling Words in Context Spelling words are missing or used inaccurately. One spelling word is used correctly, or two are used with limited context. Two spelling words are used correctly in context. Two or more spelling words are used naturally and deepen meaning.
Connection to Theme The connection to the novel or unit is unclear. The connection to theme is present but general. The poem clearly connects to a theme from the novel or unit. The poem shows deep insight into responsibility, courage, silence, or protection.

Number the Stars, Chapters 8 and 9, “There Has Been a Death” and “Why Are You Lying?”

Name:
Date:
Class:
Focus: Author’s craft. How does Lowry create tension through a sealed casket, a room of mourners, and soldiers who are suspicious?
Annotation Task
Circle one word or phrase that creates tension.
Underline one detail that feels more dangerous than it first appears.
Box one spelling word, such as casket, mourning, or suspicious. Explain how it adds to the tension.
Passage Locator

Use Chapter 9, “Why Are You Lying?”

Find the scene after the casket has arrived and people begin gathering in the living room for Great-aunt Birte’s wake.

Start: the paragraph that begins with the gleaming wooden casket resting in the center of the living room.

Stop: when Ellen returns with her parents and Peter enters the room.

Focus words to track: casket, mourning, suspicious, terrified.

Reading focus: How does Lowry create tension before the soldiers even enter the scene?

Craft Response

Lowry creates tension in this scene by _____. One word or detail that shows this is _____ because _____.
