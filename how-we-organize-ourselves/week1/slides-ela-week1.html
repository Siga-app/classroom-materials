Period 3 · Context · 4 min
Where does this story take place?
🇩🇪 Germany
Occupying power · 1940–1945
Invaded Denmark on April 9, 1940. The Nazis took control of government, police, and daily life.
🇩🇰 Denmark
Occupied — but not silent
In October 1943, ordinary Danes organized a secret rescue — ~7,000 Jewish people smuggled to Sweden by fishing boat.
🇸🇪 Sweden
Neutral · Safe harbor
Just 4 km across the Øresund Strait — close enough to cross by fishing boat in the dark.
⛵ Copenhagen → Sweden: 4 km.  A 45-minute crossing by fishing boat. Ordinary people made this happen.
What was happening in the world
1940
Germany invades Denmark. Occupation begins.
Oct 1943
Nazis plan mass arrest. Danish people find out first.
The Rescue
~7,000 Jewish Danes escape to Sweden in 3 weeks.
1945
Germany surrenders. Denmark liberated. Jews return home.
1989
Lowry publishes Number the Stars. Newbery Medal 1990.
Period 3 · Meet the Author · 3 min
Meet Lois Lowry.
Who she is
American author, born 1937. She has written over 30 books for young people and won the Newbery Medal twice — for Number the Stars (1990) and The Giver (1994).
The real story behind it
Lowry's childhood friend Annelise Platt grew up in Denmark during the occupation. Annemarie in the novel is inspired by her. Lowry visited Denmark and interviewed survivors before writing a single word.
Why she wrote it
"I wanted to tell the story of the Danish people — the ordinary ones who made extraordinary choices. The ones who helped because it was simply the right thing to do."
Period 3 · Genre · 3 min
What kind of book is this?
📚
Fiction
Invented characters and events. The story did not happen — but it could have. Annemarie, Ellen, and their families are not real people.
📰
Nonfiction
Real events, real people, real facts. A history textbook about WWII is nonfiction. It tells you what happened.
This is what we're reading
⭐
Historical Fiction
Real setting, invented characters. Denmark 1943 is real. The occupation is real. The rescue of Danish Jews is real. Annemarie's family is invented — but their experience is based on truth.
"Fiction lets us feel history from the inside. A textbook tells you what happened. A novel makes you understand what it cost."
