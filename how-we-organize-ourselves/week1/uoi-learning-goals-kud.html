UOI · Grade 5 · How We Organize Ourselves

Our Learning Goals

"The systems societies create to organize themselves reflect their values, and shape the rights and responsibilities of every individual within them."

Micah 6:8 — Act justly. Love mercy. Walk humbly.
K

Students Will KNOW

Facts, vocabulary, and content we will learn throughout the unit

1How the U.S. government is organized — three branches, the Constitution, and the Bill of Rights.
2What the Holocaust was, and what antisemitism means. (SS.5.HE.1.1)
3The causes, key events, and effects of WWII — including the German occupation of Denmark.
4What makes a historical recount different from a story — and how to write one.
5Key vocabulary: democracy, occupation, propaganda, resistance, antisemitism, bystander, rescuer, upstander, legislation, veto.
6Math: order of operations, numerical patterns & input/output tables, LCM & GCF, and integers.
U

Students Will UNDERSTAND That

Big ideas and lasting understandings that go beyond this unit

1The systems societies build reflect their values — and they protect or fail their people based on those values. (Central Idea)
2Democratic governments are designed to protect individual rights — but those protections depend on citizens who act with courage and principle.
3History's greatest injustices happened when systems of power were built around hatred and dehumanization — and when ordinary people chose to stay silent.
4Every individual within a system has rights AND responsibilities. The choice between bystander and upstander is a moral, civic, and spiritual one.
5Mathematical patterns and algebraic thinking are tools for organizing the world — just as laws and constitutions are tools for organizing society.
D

Students Will Be Able To DO

Skills and actions we will practice and demonstrate

1Analyze literary texts and historical sources to find theme, character development, and author's perspective.
2Compare and contrast primary and secondary sources on the same historical topic.
3Write a historically grounded recount using narrative techniques, descriptive detail, and accurate sequencing.
4Use critical thinking to evaluate the ethical and civic dimensions of historical events.
5Solve multi-step real-world problems using algebraic reasoning — including writing, evaluating, and interpreting expressions and equations.
6Take principled, caring action through service — Letters Across Time.
