Period 7 · Grouping Symbols · 10 min
Work from the inside out.
When you see parentheses inside brackets — always start with the innermost group.
(3 + 4) × (12 ÷ (2 + 1))
A rescue operation organised 3 boats. Each boat carried (12 ÷ (2+1)) families.
Write and evaluate the expression for the total number of families rescued.
1
Innermost parentheses first
(2 + 1) = 3
2
Work outward
12 ÷ 3 = 4 → (3 + 4) × 4 = 7 × 4 = 28
True or False? · 10 min
4 × (3 + 5) = 4 × 3 + 5 × 4
Evaluate both sides independently. Then compare.

You cannot just circle T or F — you must write:
"This equation is [true/false] because the left side equals ___ and the right side equals ___."
LP · Principled
"In mathematics, as in government — you cannot claim something is true without evidence."
A principled mathematician shows their work and their reasoning.
Not just the answer — the why.

Period 15 · GCF · 15 min
Greatest Common Factor.
The largest factor that divides evenly into two numbers.
🌳
Method 1
Factor Trees
Break each number into its prime factors. Find what they share.
📋
Method 2
List the Factors
List all factors of each number. Find the largest one they have in common.
Try both methods for GCF(36, 24). Which is more efficient — and why?
Period 15 · LCM · 12 min
Least Common Multiple.
The smallest multiple that two numbers share.
Multiples of 4:
4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24...
Multiples of 6:
6, 12, 18, 24...
💬 Connect to P12: The LCM is the first point where two input/output patterns meet.
